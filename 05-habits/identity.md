# Identity

> "Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you wish to become. No single instance will transform your beliefs, but as the votes build up, so does the evidence of your new identity."
> — James Clear, *Atomic Habits*

Your habits don't define your future—they reveal who you're becoming right now. Each morning you wake up early, you vote for the person who owns their day. Each time you ship work, you vote for the builder. Each rep, each page, each hard conversation—these are not tasks. They are identity evidence.

Goals tell you where you want to go. Systems tell you how you'll get there. Identity tells you who is making the journey.

## Purpose

### Why does this matter?

> "He who has a why can bear almost any how."
> — Viktor Frankl

The identity statements above are only worth maintaining if they connect to something larger. When discipline wavers, the question isn't "am I motivated?"—it's "do I remember why?"

Write your answer here. Make it personal and specific—not aspirational language, but the real reason:

*[Your why here — who are you doing this for, what are you building toward, what does the person you're becoming make possible?]*
