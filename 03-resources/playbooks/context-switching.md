# Context Switching Protocol

Context switching is expensive. Research from the University of California, Irvine found it takes an average of 23 minutes to fully refocus after an interruption. Most knowledge workers switch contexts 30+ times per day — which means they may never reach deep focus at all.

This playbook exists to make switching intentional, fast, and low-cost.

---

## The Core Principle

Switching is not the enemy. Unmanaged switching is. A planned transition between CTO mode and Creator mode costs 2 minutes. An unplanned interruption from a Slack notification costs 23 minutes plus whatever momentum was lost.

**Design your day so switches are scheduled, not reactive.**

---

## Time-of-Day Theming

Align work type to your cognitive state. From Daniel Pink's *When*: most people follow a Peak → Trough → Recovery arc across the day.

| Time Block | State | Best Work Type |
|------------|-------|----------------|
| Morning (peak) | Highest alertness, best mood | Deep work — writing, complex analysis, strategy, code |
| Midday (trough) | Energy dip, mood dip | Admin — email, scheduling, simple tasks, meetings |
| Afternoon (recovery) | Rebounding energy, looser thinking | Creative work — brainstorming, connecting ideas, editing |

**Protect the peak.** The morning hours are the most valuable cognitive resource you have. Guard them against meetings, Slack, and reactive work.

---

## Day-of-Week Theming

Batch similar activities into dedicated days or half-days to eliminate small, frequent switches.

**Example structure:**

| Day | Primary Theme |
|-----|---------------|
| Monday | Planning + strategy (set the week's direction) |
| Tuesday | Deep work — CTO (engineering, architecture, product) |
| Wednesday | Meetings + 1:1s (batch all people time here) |
| Thursday | Deep work — Creator (writing, content, Cipher) |
| Friday | Review + admin + weekly close |

Adjust to your actual calendar constraints. The goal is batching, not perfection.

## The 2-Minute Exit Ritual

Always do this before leaving a context — before a meeting, before switching hats, before ending a session.

1. **Capture where you stopped** — Write one sentence: "I was doing X. The next step is Y." This is your re-entry note.
2. **Write the next action explicitly** — Not "work on this" but "open file X and write section Y."
3. **Close all related tabs, windows, and files** — A clean desktop is a clean mind. Don't leave breadcrumbs that pull you back.
4. **Take 3 slow breaths** — Signals to your nervous system that the previous context is complete.
5. **Physically move if possible** — Stand up, get water, walk to another room. Physical state change accelerates mental state change.

---

## The 2-Minute Entry Ritual

Always do this before starting a new context block.

1. **Read your context note** — The one you wrote in the exit ritual. This is why you wrote it.
2. **Set one clear intention** — "In this block, I will [specific outcome]." Not a task list — one outcome.
3. **Start with the hardest part** — Resistance is highest at the beginning. The first five minutes determine the quality of the session.
4. **Close everything unrelated** — Email, Slack, unrelated browser tabs. Create the conditions for focus before you need focus.

---

## Emergency Interruption Protocol

When an unavoidable interruption hits mid-flow:

1. **Write "INTERRUPTED:" at the top of your working doc** and capture your exact state in 1-2 sentences — what you were doing, what the open question was, where you were headed.
2. **Handle the interruption completely** — Don't half-attend to it. Give it full attention so it resolves cleanly.
3. **Return and read your INTERRUPTED note** — This is your recovery mechanism.
4. **Budget the 23 minutes** — Accept that refocus takes time and don't rush it. The first five minutes back may feel sluggish.

## Protected Time Rules

From Cal Newport's *Deep Work*: the ability to focus without distraction is becoming rare and increasingly valuable.

**Calendar discipline:**
- Block deep work on your calendar like a meeting with your CEO. Label it "Deep Work — [topic]" so others see it's intentional, not open space.
- Set minimum block sizes: deep work requires at least 90 minutes to reach meaningful depth. Anything shorter is warm-up without payoff.
- Protect the first 90 minutes of your morning as non-negotiable deep work. Schedule no meetings before 10am when possible.

**Notification discipline:**
- During deep work blocks: phone on Do Not Disturb, Slack on pause, email closed.
- Designate two response windows per day (e.g., 12pm and 4pm) for async communication.
- "I'll respond during my next communication window" is a complete sentence.

**Meeting discipline:**
- Schedule all recurring meetings on the same 1-2 days per week.
- Batch ad-hoc meetings to your designated meeting days rather than scattering them.
- A meeting in the middle of a deep work day costs more than the meeting's duration.

## Mode-Specific Switching: CTO ↔ Creator

This operating system serves two primary roles. Switching between them requires an intentional hat change.

**Exiting CTO mode:**
- Log any open threads in the team tracker or Waiting For list
- Send any pending replies or pings before fully closing
- Write your exit note: what was the last CTO decision made, what's pending

**Entering Creator mode:**
- Silence Slack and work Slack
- Open your content queue or writing doc
- Set one output target for this session ("publish one draft" or "write 500 words")

**Exiting Creator mode:**
- Save all drafts
- Note which pieces are in flight and their next state
- Write your exit note

**Entering CTO mode:**
- Review today's team priorities
- Check Slack briefly for anything urgent
- Set your CTO intention for this block

## When You Feel Pulled in All Directions

This is a signal, not a state. Run this triage:

1. **List everything competing for attention** — Put it on paper. It's never as many things as it feels.
2. **Identify the ONE most important item** — Not the most urgent. The most important.
3. **Schedule everything else** — Give each item a time slot. "I'll get to that at 3pm" removes it from active RAM.
4. **Work the ONE thing.**
