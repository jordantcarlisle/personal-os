# Learning System

> Connect your educational background, reading list, and curriculum to a Learning Tutor agent that accelerates mastery across domains.

**Status:** Not activated

To activate this module, run `/onboard` and select "Learning System", or follow the manual setup guide in the docs.

---

## What Gets Set Up

- Curriculum map defining your polymath architecture — the domains you're building toward mastery in
- Sources inventory mapping all active courses, books, and learning materials to curriculum tiers
- Active study plan with quarterly focus areas and weekly study block targets
- Learning Tutor agent with access to your curriculum, reading list, and Readwise highlights
- `/study` command for on-demand Socratic sessions, quizzes, and concept exploration
- Study block awareness baked into `/plan` — agent checks whether your evening study block is protected before committing the day
- Learning connections surfaced in `/reflect` — yesterday's work mapped to curriculum systems, interdisciplinary links called out

---

## What Your Agent Does For You

**Spaced repetition and active recall:**
> "You read the Kropotkin chapter on mutual aid five days ago. Let's do a quick retrieval check: without looking at your notes, what was his core argument against Huxley's 'gladiatorial theory' of evolution? Take 60 seconds, then I'll give you the source passage."

**Interdisciplinary connections surfaced across domains:**
> "The Adam Smith / Kropotkin convergence you noted on natural sympathy maps directly to System 5 — this is essentially an early version of the debate between homo economicus and behavioral economics. Kahneman's System 1/2 framework is a direct descendant of what Smith called 'fellow feeling.' Want me to add this to your manifesto research?"

**Socratic study sessions on demand:**
> "You've been studying evolutionary biology for three weeks. Let me push on this: if natural selection operates at the individual gene level (Dawkins), how do you reconcile that with Kropotkin's group-selection argument? What's the weakest point in Kropotkin's case?"

**Curriculum mapping to your daily work:**
> "Today's engineering architecture review maps to System 2, Tier 3 — distributed systems design. Your 1:1 with the team maps to System 4 — leadership and organizational psychology. You're getting curriculum credit without opening a textbook."

## Source Materials

The Learning Tutor agent draws principles from:

- *Make It Stick* — Peter Brown, Henry Roediger, Mark McDaniel (retrieval practice, interleaving, spaced repetition, the illusion of knowing)
- *Range* — David Epstein (the case for generalism, breadth before depth, conceptual transfer across domains)
- *Ultralearning* — Scott Young (directness, drill design, feedback loops, aggressive self-directed learning)
- *A Mind for Numbers* — Barbara Oakley (focused vs. diffuse thinking, chunking, defeating procrastination on hard material)
