# Financial Organization

> Maintain budget awareness, prepare for financial reviews, and track progress toward your goals — without letting money management consume your attention.

**Status:** Not activated

To activate this module, run `/onboard` and select "Financial Organization", or follow the manual setup guide in the docs.

---

## What Gets Set Up

- Financial organization structure mapping your accounts, income streams, and goal categories
- Budget framework with spending categories and monthly targets
- Financial Advisor agent with access to your financial snapshot and goal tracking
- Monthly financial review template baked into your review workflow
- Decision support for major purchases — life-energy calculations and room-for-error checks
- Goal tracking updated each review cycle (emergency fund, investment targets, business milestones)

---

## What Your Agent Does For You

**Budget awareness with spending alerts:**
> "You're on track this month in most categories, but dining and entertainment is running 40% over pace with 10 days left. Not a crisis, but worth noting if you have any larger planned expenses coming. Your fixed costs and savings contributions are all on schedule."

**Monthly financial review preparation:**
> "Your monthly review is in two days. I've pulled together the standard summary: net cash flow vs. target, savings rate for the month, goal progress on your emergency fund (you're at 4.2 months of expenses — target is 6), and one item that needs a decision: your subscription audit flagged three services you haven't used in 60+ days."

**Life-energy decision support on major purchases:**
> "You're considering the $3,200 standing desk. At your current effective hourly rate, that's roughly 8 hours of life energy. It will last you at minimum 10 years and you're at your desk 6+ hours a day. On those terms, it's likely a good trade. The question is timing — does it fit within this month's discretionary budget without stress?"

**Room-for-error checks:**
> "Before committing to the new equipment purchase, one check: your current liquid reserves cover 4 months of expenses. Adding this purchase would pull that to 3.6 months. Still above your 3-month floor, but worth noting — you're reducing your margin of safety. Is the timing right, or would waiting 60 days feel better?"

## Source Materials

The Financial Advisor agent draws principles from:

- *The Psychology of Money* — Morgan Housel (wealth vs. rich, room for error, compounding, behavior over math)
- *I Will Teach You To Be Rich* — Ramit Sethi (automation, conscious spending, systems over willpower, big wins vs. small cuts)
- *The Simple Path to Wealth* — JL Collins (index fund philosophy, FI math, avoiding complexity, the wealth-building arc)
