# Career & Role Management

> Stay clear on your role, prepared for every meeting, and deliberate about the career capital you're building.

**Status:** Not activated

To activate this module, run `/onboard` and select "Career & Role Management", or follow the manual setup guide in the docs.

---

## What Gets Set Up

- Role definition document — your scope, current priorities, success metrics, and how you're evaluated
- Team map with direct reports, key stakeholders, and relationship context
- Stakeholder registry tracking commitments made, commitments received, and update cadence
- Career Coach agent with access to your role definition, team map, and stakeholder registry
- Meeting prep compiled automatically during `/triage` before 1:1s and key stakeholder calls
- Debrief intelligence logged after significant meetings — decisions made, commitments captured, context preserved

---

## What Your Agent Does For You

**1:1 prep compiled before you walk in:**
> "You have your 1:1 with Marcus in 30 minutes. Prep summary: Last week he flagged a blocker on the API integration — status unknown since then, worth checking. He's been leading the authentication refactor for 3 weeks and hasn't mentioned it in standup — could be heads-down or stuck. His energy has been lower than normal the past two weeks. One open item from you: you said you'd share the vendor comparison doc by Friday. Did that go out?"

**Stakeholder update tracking:**
> "You have three stakeholders who are past their expected update cadence: the board (last update 6 weeks ago, typically 4), your CPO (last sync 12 days ago, you typically connect weekly), and the design lead on the new feature (no update since kickoff 3 weeks ago). Which one is most at risk?"

**Career capital tracking:**
> "This quarter you've shipped the data pipeline overhaul, led the vendor selection process, and hired two engineers. That's execution capital. What you're light on: thought leadership and external visibility. No posts published, no conference submissions, no external talks. If your thesis is to be known as a builder-thinker, you're accumulating on one side of the ledger only."

**Meeting debrief intelligence:**
> "Quick debrief capture from your board meeting: three decisions were made (architecture direction, Q2 hiring pause, partnership evaluation timeline). Two commitments you made: deliver financial model update by April 15, connect CFO with the legal contact. Two items you're now waiting on: board approval of the revised roadmap, and the chair's intro to their network contact. Want me to log these to your Waiting For list?"

## Source Materials

The Career Coach agent draws principles from:

- *High Output Management* — Andrew Grove (managerial leverage, 1:1 frameworks, output vs. activity, the role of a manager as multiplier)
- *So Good They Can't Ignore You* — Cal Newport (career capital theory, craftsman mindset, deliberate practice, the danger of passion-first thinking)
- *Radical Candor* — Kim Scott (caring personally and challenging directly, the feedback quadrants, creating psychological safety for real conversations)
