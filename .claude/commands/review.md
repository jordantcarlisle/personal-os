# Chief of Staff — Weekly Review

The most important ritual in the system. Walk through it systematically.

## Before Starting

Block 60-90 minutes. This is not a quick check-in. Read `00-cockpit/weekly-review.md` for the full template.

## Part 1: Get Clear (20 min)

1. **Process inbox to zero:** Read every item in `00-cockpit/inbox.md`. For each, walk through the GTD processing checklist. Every item gets a destination — nothing stays.

2. **Mind sweep:** Ask prompting questions to surface open loops:
   - "Anything you told someone you'd do this week?"
   - "Anything nagging at the back of your mind?"
   - "Any commitments from meetings this week?"
   - "Any personal errands or tasks you've been avoiding?"
   Capture all new items to inbox, then process them too.

3. **Calendar review:** Review this week's past events for follow-ups. Check next week for prep needs.

## Part 2: Get Current (20 min)

4. **Review every active project** in `01-projects/`:
   - Does it have a clear next action? If not, define one now.
   - Is it stalled? Kill, pause, or pivot?
   - Is the status field accurate?
   - Update the Active Projects Radar on dashboard.

5. **Review areas** in `02-areas/`: Any neglected? Any consuming too much time?

6. **Review Waiting For** on dashboard: Flag anything >2 weeks. Suggest follow-ups. Remove resolved items.

7. **Review Someday/Maybe** (if exists): Anything to activate? Delete?

## Part 3: Get Creative (15 min)

8. **Reflect:**
   - "What was your biggest win this week?"
   - "What was the biggest lesson?"
   - "What went well that you should keep doing?"
   - "What didn't work that you should change?"

9. **Happiness check:** "On a scale of 1-10, how happy were you this week? Why?"

## Part 4: Commit (15 min)

10. **Non-negotiables** for next week (max 3)
11. **Say NO:** What will you actively decline or defer?
12. **Weekly theme:** One phrase capturing next week's focus
13. **Update dashboard** for Monday
14. **Save:** Run `/save` to commit and push all changes
