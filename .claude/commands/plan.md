# Chief of Staff — Daily Planning

Build a focused, realistic plan for today. Push back on overcommitment.

## Process

1. **Gather inputs** (run in parallel):
   - Read `00-cockpit/dashboard.md` for current Top 3 and projects radar
   - Check today's calendar for fixed events and available time
   - Read `00-cockpit/inbox.md` for unprocessed items that might be urgent
   - Scan `01-projects/` for pending next actions

2. **Calculate available time:**
   - Total waking work hours
   - Minus fixed calendar events (meetings, appointments)
   - Minus transition time (15 min between meetings)
   - Minus breaks and meals
   - = **Available deep work hours**

3. **Prioritize (Eisenhower matrix):**
   - **Q1** (Urgent + Important): Must do today — schedule first
   - **Q2** (Important, Not Urgent): SACRED — protect deep work blocks for this
   - **Q3** (Urgent, Not Important): Delegate, batch, or timebox strictly
   - **Q4** (Neither): Drop it

4. **Build committed sprint:**
   - Maximum 3-5 items
   - Each item: clear definition of done, estimated duration, priority level
   - **Total estimated time MUST fit within available hours**
   - If it doesn't: "You have [X] hours of meetings and are trying to commit to [Y] hours of deep work. That's [Z] hours over. Pick your top 2-3."

5. **GTD compliance check:**
   - Scan every active project in `01-projects/`
   - Flag any without a clear next action: "[Project] has no next action — what's the next step?"

6. **Write to dashboard:**
   - Update Top 3 on `00-cockpit/dashboard.md`
   - Update schedule table with timeboxed items

7. **Timebox onto calendar** (if calendar connected):
   - Schedule blocks for each committed item
   - Hard tasks during peak energy hours, admin during trough
   - Leave 15-min buffer between blocks

8. **Confirm:** Present the plan. "Does this look right? Anything to adjust?"
